Interview Prep: Module 1: Course Overview

Here are some common interview questions related to the AWS Course Overview module, along with suggested answers.

1. What is cloud computing and why is it beneficial for businesses?

Answer: Cloud computing is the on-demand delivery of IT resources over the Internet with pay-as-you-go pricing. Benefits include: Cost-effectiveness (trade CapEx for OpEx), Scalability & Elasticity (scale up/down as needed), Agility (faster resource provisioning), Global Reach (deploy globally in minutes), and Reduced Operational Burden (AWS manages infrastructure).

2. What is AWS and what makes it a leading cloud provider?

Answer: AWS (Amazon Web Services) is the world's most comprehensive and broadly adopted cloud platform. It's a leader due to its breadth and depth of services (over 200), extensive global infrastructure (Regions, AZs, Edge Locations), strong focus on innovation, and a robust ecosystem of partners and tools.

3. Explain the concept of the AWS Free Tier. Why is it important?

Answer: The AWS Free Tier allows new and existing customers to explore and try out AWS services free of charge up to certain limits for a specified period (e.g., 12 months free, always free offers). It's important because it enables hands-on learning and experimentation without incurring costs, helping users understand services before committing financially.

4. What are some common career paths in AWS?

Answer: Common career paths include Cloud Architect, Cloud Engineer, DevOps Engineer, Solutions Architect, Cloud Security Engineer, and Cloud Developer. These roles often require expertise in designing, implementing, and managing cloud solutions.

5. Why is it crucial to set up billing alerts in your AWS account?

Answer: Setting up billing alerts is crucial to avoid unexpected charges. It notifies you when your estimated AWS charges exceed a predefined threshold, allowing you to monitor your spending, especially when using the Free Tier or experimenting with new services, and take corrective action if necessary.

6. What is the AWS Shared Responsibility Model? (Briefly)

Answer: It defines what AWS is responsible for (security OF the Cloud - infrastructure, hardware, software, facilities) and what the customer is responsible for (security IN the Cloud - data, applications, OS, network configuration, IAM). It's a partnership for security.

7. How would you approach learning a new AWS service?

Answer: Start with the official AWS documentation and whitepapers. Utilize the AWS Free Tier for hands-on experimentation. Look for tutorials, online courses, and community forums. Understand its core purpose, key features, pricing model, and how it integrates with other AWS services.

8. What are the advantages of a pay-as-you-go model in cloud computing?

Answer: Advantages include: No upfront capital expenditure, only pay for resources consumed, ability to scale resources up or down without wasted capacity, and cost optimization by aligning spending with actual usage.

9. What are some common AWS certifications and why pursue them?

Answer: Common certifications include Cloud Practitioner (foundational), Solutions Architect Associate/Professional, Developer Associate, SysOps Administrator Associate, and various Specialty certifications. Pursuing them validates your cloud expertise, enhances career opportunities, and demonstrates a commitment to continuous learning in the AWS ecosystem.

10. Describe a scenario where cloud computing would be a disadvantage.

Answer: While rare, potential disadvantages could include: very specific regulatory compliance requirements that mandate on-premises data, extremely low-latency applications where physical proximity to hardware is critical, or situations where existing on-premises investments are so significant that migration costs outweigh cloud benefits in the short term. Also, potential vendor lock-in if not managed carefully.
